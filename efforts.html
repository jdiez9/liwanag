ELECTRIFICATION EFFORTS

Ekwalidad at Ekonomiya


The Philippine government has made continual efforts to push for nationwide electrification, yet until now these efforts have not reached the geographically-isolated parts of the nation. Far-flung rural areas and mountainous regions continue to struggle with limited or unreliable access to electricity. Under the presidency of Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo in 2004, the National Electrification Administration (NEA) was established. The purpose of this commission was to aid in the electrification of "all barangays." The program during her administration was the Expanded Rural Electrification Program. In 2011, when President Benigno S. Aquino III assumed the presidency, he spearheaded the Sitio Electrification Program, aimed at delivering electricity to all "sitios" of the country. WATCH: Microgrid Systems Act Signed into Law by PRRD

"There is a need to utilize rapid advancements in technology such as microgrids, distributed energy resources, and other alternative service providers suitable for unserved and unviable areas."
- President Rodrigo R. Duterte -

In 2022, President Duterte signed into law a bill to vitalize microgrid development in underserved areas nationwide. This measure aligns with the government's goal to achieve full electrification of the country. This law works in accordance with the Department of Energy (DOE) to use microgrid systems, an alternative form of power supply, to reach those areas without electricity access such as mountainous regions and far-flung communities. As of March 2025, several microgrid systems are already serving households in Palawan, Cebu, Camarines Sur, and Davao Occidental.
